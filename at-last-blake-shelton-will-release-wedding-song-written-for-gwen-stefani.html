Blake Shelton and Gwen Stefani finally tied the knot this July, and one of the most interesting tidbits to come out of the wedding was the fact that Shelton wrote a song for his new wife.

If you’ve been wanting to hear the song for yourself, this is your lucky day. Shelton will be releasing it as one of the bonus tracks on the upcoming deluxe edition of his latest album, Body Language.

Blake Shelton to Release Wedding Song for Gwen Stefani

The Voice host Carson Daly officiated Shelton and Stefani’s wedding, and he later spilled details about the nuptials. The couple wrote their own vows, and Shelton’s took the form of a song.

“Blake starts by saying, ‘You know Gwen’s always giving me a hard time about not writing enough songs so I wrote a song,’” Daly shared. “Guitar comes in frame, stool, next thing you know he wrote her a song and he sings her a song while he’s crying that he wrote specifically for her.”

Shelton revealed in July that he had recorded the song, called “We Can Reach the Stars,” and wanted to release it publicly at some point. He also shared that he had the help of his friend Craig Wiseman to write the song.

Fans will be able to hear the composition on the deluxe edition of Shelton’s most recent album Body Language. The release will feature four bonus tracks, including Shelton’s recent single “Come Back as a Country Boy.”

When Is Shelton’s Deluxe ‘Body Language’ Album Coming Out?

The deluxe edition of Shelton’s album Body Language will be available December 3, and you can pre-order or pre-save it now. In addition to “We Can Reach the Stars” and “Come Back as a Country Boy,” the deluxe album will also feature collaborations with some fellow country stars.

The track “Fire Up the Night” will feature singer Hardy, while “Throw It on Back” is a collaboration with Brooks & Dunn. According to the tracklist, “Come Back as a Country Boy” will open the album, while Shelton’s wedding song for Stefani will close it.

“I think it’s something I want to share, because I’m really proud of it,” Shelton told SiriusXM’s Storme Warren of his song for Stefani. “It is, for me, something that I want people to hear and know, because I’m proud to be married to Gwen, and so I’m proud of that song.”
