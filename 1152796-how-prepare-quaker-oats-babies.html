Things to note when preparing oatmeals for babies

  • Adding peanut butter to oatmeal is key for avoiding food allergies in a baby.
  • Discuss with your pediatrician about introducing peanuts to your little one.
  • Use peanut butter with only one ingredient (peanuts) to avoid extra oil, salt, or sugar.
  • You can also use almond, cashew and sunflower seed butter.
  • You can add more water, mother's milk or cow milk to thin it out before serving.
  • Use fresh mother's milk or what you have refrigerated for about three days or less.
  • Store leftover oats in an air-tight container in the fridge for up to 4 days.
  • You can mix oatmeal or oatmeal formula into your baby's porridge and puddings.
