You need to know this about almond oil

Almond oil is obtained from the seeds of sweet almond (Prunus dulcis var. Dulcis) and also from the bitter almond (Prunus dulcis var. Amara). The bitter almond oil contains the glycoside amygdalin, from which toxic hydrocyanic acid forms. Such oil is not suitable for consumption.

After removing the hydrocyanic acid, people use it in the production of perfume, cosmetics, in soap production and as a food additive. The sweet almond oil is an oil of the highest quality, used for cooking. Sweet almond oil is effectively used for beauty and skin care products and in the fields of medicine as well.

How to use almond oil for skin whitening

Almond seeds can be used for home beauty treatments as they are (the form of grains) or in the liquid state (as an oil).

You can use 100% skin lightening oil or combine it with other natural ingredients to obtain excellent, easy-to-use, and very economical solutions for the healthy and good-looking skin. You can add it to skin lightening gels, creams and scrubs to whiten and cleanse the skin, remove stains and get rid of dead skin cells. In comparison with coconut oil (which tends to clog pores of most people), almond oil is non-camedogenic.
