'DWTS' is Getting Ready for Disney 100 Night Heres What to Expect

Posted by Tandra Barner on Saturday, August 17, 2024

TALENTRECAP.COM Talent Dancing With The Stars

Disney/Eric McCandless

Reading Time: 2 minutes

Next week, fans can expect to see Dancing With The Stars’ traditional Disney Night, with a bit of a change. This season’s Disney Night promises to be bigger and better than ever before, as DWTS celebrates Walt Disney’s 100th Anniversary.

DWTS to Celebrate 100 Years of Walt Disney Studios

Next week’s Disney 100 Night is going to be a special episode for the hit ABC series. Celebrity pairs will once again return to the ballroom with songs from classic Disney Films. It’s also totally expected that pairs will dress up as Disney characters.

The next episode will open up with with the DWTS performing to “We Don’t Talk About Bruno” from Encanto. This opening number will be choreographed by Christopher Scott. Within the episode, fans should expect to see celebrity pairs dancing Contemporary, Paso Doble, Jazz, Quickstep, Viennese Waltz, or Waltz.

Disney 100 Night will air on Tuesday 7th, October 2023, at 8:00-10:01 p.m. EDT/PDT. It will be simulcasting LIVE across both ABC and Disney+ in local time zones. It will then be available the next day on Hulu.

Songs, Dance Styles Revealed

Film star Alyson Hannigan and pro Sasha Farber will perform a Jazz Routine to “Be Our Guest” from Beauty and the Beast. Barry Williams and pro Peta Murgatroyd will be doing the same dance style to “He’s a Tramp” from Lady and the Tramp.

Meanwhile, Mira Sorvino and pro Gleb Savchenko will perform a Waltz to “A Dream Is a Wish Your Heart Makes” from Cinderella. Former Bachelorette Charity Lawson and pro Artem Chigvintsev will be performing a Viennese Waltz to “Part of Your World” from The Little Mermaid. While NFL star Adrian Peterson and pro Britt Stewart will be performing in the same style, to “Baby Mine” from Dumbo.

There will be two paso dobles of the night. One will be performed to “Un Poco Loco” from Coco, by Xochitl Gomez and Val Chmerkovskiy. The other will be performed by Emma Slater and Mauricio Umansky to “The Sorcerer’s Apprentice” from Fantasia.

Vanderpump Rules star Ariana Madix and pro Pasha Pashkov will perform a Contemporary to “Into the Unknown” from Frozen II. While, Lele Pons and pro Brandon Armstrong will perform a Rumba to “Can You Feel the Love Tonight” from The Lion King.

Finally, Jason Mraz and Daniella Karagach will perform a Foxtrot to “A Whole New World” from Aladdin. Harry Jowsey and Rylee Arnold will perform a Quickstep to “You’ve Got a Friend in Me” from Toy Story.
